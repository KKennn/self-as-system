Project Description

This project, Self as System, is a â€œself-portraitâ€ about my online presence on Instagram. The portraiture expression is based on the comprehension and manipulation of the user data downloaded from Instagram. The information I use from the downloaded data package includes direct message histories, comment entries, new connections with other Instagram users, contents Iâ€™ve seen, contents Iâ€™ve liked, and the ads interests of mine. These pieces of data are more representative of my personalities, preferences, and identities than others like the location, login histories, etc. The time frame of collected data ranges from 2018 to 2020, when this project was created.
Self as System consists of five individual sketches derived from different types of data, which come together as a whole non-linear, systems-based self-portrait of my online presence.
The first sketch is a list of color-coded Instagram Ads Interests. They are all generated by my online activities, but not all of them I agree with, or even am aware of. Those Ads Interests I agree I have are labelled green; those I find inaccurate red; those even I myself am not sure if I have yellow. This sketch leads me to a reflection of my personal agency in the digital world. Is it still my power to decide what I could, would, or should â€œlike?â€
The second sketch contains all emojis I have used to comment others' posts for the past few years in reverse chronological order. Interestingly, some clusters of the same emojis actually belong to different, consecutively made comments. I reckon it shows my state of mind/mood during a time period.
The third sketch is a bar of five vowels. The ratio of the area shows the frequency I have used each vowel for commenting others' posts for the past few years. This sketch might not only portray a personal language usage habit but a general linguistic phenomenon.
The fourth sketch is a selfie with adjustment of image threshold level, which makes it "binary" with only black and white pixels. Then I filled the black area with all the DMs sent by me. Among all the data, I believe the direct messages I send to my friends can best represent me.
The fifth sketch is a data visualization chart. The bubbles are a visualization of my Instagram activities in the past three years. The larger the bubble is the more activities there are. Both the variety of the level of activity in different years and in different forms are shown.
